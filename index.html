About me

I am Siming LIU (柳思铭[ljow˨˩.s̩˥.mjɤŋ˧˥]), a second-year master student in the Department of Linguistics at Beijing Language and Culture University.

My research interest lies in syntax and its interfaces with semantics and morphology. Broadly, I care about the taxonomy and motivation of mismatches between form and meaning in human languages. Specifically, I am interested in A/A'-dependencies, locality constraints, linearization, multiple exponence and the structure of nominal phrases. Currently I focus on cross-linguistic non-nominal movement with A-properties.

Besides the above theoretical issues, I am also interested in understudied languages in China as well as around the world.
